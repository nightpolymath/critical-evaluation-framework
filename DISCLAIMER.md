# Disclaimer

## Use of the Critical Evaluation Framework

The Critical Evaluation Framework (CEF) is provided as-is for educational, research, and analytical purposes. It is a tool for systematic interrogation of claims and arguments—not a substitute for domain expertise, peer review, or professional judgment.

### What the CEF Does

- Provides a structured methodology for identifying reasoning failures and epistemic weaknesses
- Helps practitioners audit their own or others' analytical work
- Surfaces assumptions, causal claims, and evidentiary gaps
- Does not replace expert evaluation or disciplinary knowledge

### What the CEF Does NOT Do

- Guarantee correctness or eliminate analytical error
- Substitute for professional advice (legal, financial, medical, technical, etc.)
- Provide definitive answers to empirical questions
- Eliminate the need for human judgment and expert review
- Serve as the sole basis for high-stakes decisions

### LLM Use

If using the CEF with large language models (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, etc.):
- LLMs are capable but fallible reasoning assistants
- Their outputs require independent verification and expert validation
- Do not rely solely on LLM analysis for consequential decisions
- Cross-reference multiple sources and domain experts

### Limitations

The CEF addresses systematic reasoning failures but cannot:
- Detect all forms of bias or error
- Guarantee domain-specific accuracy
- Account for novel failure modes not yet catalogued
- Substitute for empirical validation in specific fields

### Responsibility

Users of the CEF are responsible for:
- Adapting the framework to their specific context and discipline
- Exercising judgment about which lenses apply and how
- Validating findings against domain expertise
- Taking appropriate caution with consequential claims
